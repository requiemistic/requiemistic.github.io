Upbringing

Clad in soiled rags meant to pass for garments, Finneas and his beggarly family lived an impoverished existence. Though they did have a home, per se, one would not be amiss to claim that was no description for the dilapidated shanty they spent their days residing in. What made their condition worse was the paltry amount of coin Finneas' father managed to procure from begging in nearby Kronwikk. The meager sum of coin Finneas' father managed to bring in was hardly enough to feed the family, and so the Ferntaker clan resorted to consuming rats that co-inhabited their hovel. Living amongst a rat's nest may have been a truly terrifying and disgusting existence for Finneas, but he took solace in knowing that he, at least, would not starve anytime soon as the rats seemingly bred like rabbits.

Many a day passed, and with every dawn Finneas' imagination brought, albeit little, peace to Finneas' worried mind. He would dream of better morrows, and of long, beautiful dining tables lavished with incredible feasts surrounded by friends and family. His recurrent thoughts of a greater life were cut short one day when he awoke to discover his mother had succumbed to a plague infesting many of the peasants in the surrounding area. Desperate, Finneas' father spent what little coin he had managed to scrape together to purchase a salve from a traveling alchemist, however, the medicine proved too expensive for them to afford and so, eventually, Finneas' mother yielded her life to the plague.

The coming years were a difficult and trying time for Finneas. His father showed him the ways of a beggar, instructing him in the ways of persuasion and acting in hopes of procuring even more money through these means. Though they did increase their wealth marginally, they were by no means living comfortably. What little they made, his father spent on booze as a means to suppress his grief for losing his wife. Something inside Finneas began to change, however, and through all of the grief and sorrow he had been experiencing he managed to find a light, so to speak. The light, for Finneas, was ambition. He turned his energy toward more positive things like learning a bit of alchemy. The alchemist who had moved nearby taught Finneas, perhaps as a way of apologizing for being unable to sell his father the medicine to save his mother. Perhaps it was because he saw promise in a young Finneas. Either way, Finneas learned a great deal from his mentor, and vowed to do all in his power to avoid being in a similar position as he was when he lost his mother to sickness.

Fenden

Finneas began to grow bored of his life. He wanted so much more, needed so much more. A fresh start, he thought, and he decided to search for it in a place called Fenden. And so, he set out from Kronwikk and traveled to Fenden. Finneas had already established a modest understanding of alchemy, and sought to implement his craft in ways to assist him in the life of an adventurer. The journey was long, for Finneas, at least, having never traveled a great deal prior to this journey, and eventually he would arrive in Fenden. It is here Finneas would meet a man, and eventual friend, Wytt. Finneas began working for Wytt as means of acquiring some income, initially, but eventually became closer with him. Unbeknownst to Finneas, Wytt had been hiding a secret from Finneas and as they grew closer and Wytt began to trust Finneas more, he would divulge this secret. Wytt was in fact spear-heading a rebellion against a major player in Drekis who went by the name Major Tavington. Wytt had suspected the Major was corrupt and committing heinous crimes in an attempt to wrestle more power and influence. Wytt, who was truly a man of the people, a man of justice, sought out means to prove the Major's guilt and ameliorate the country by eliminating those who would distort the safety of the citizens. Alas, Finneas had finally found a reason to be. Finneas latched onto Wytt's notion of justice and set out on an adventure to ascertain the evidence Wytt required to expose the Major for his vile discretions.
